We're releasing AIOS in the open. This is the first post, so it's worth saying
plainly what it is.

AIOS is an AI-powered operating system for agentic teams. Every person gets a
structured agent workspace. The team gets one shared brain that learns how you
work and helps you collaborate. It's MIT-licensed and self-hosted, and you
decide what leaves your machine.

It's two repos, one system. There is no third moving part to manage.

## Why we built it

We built AIOS the way most useful tools get built: by hitting the same wall
over and over.

Agents move about ten times faster than the teams around them. That speed is
real, but it breaks the things teams rely on. Context bloats. Tools entangle.
Everyone is running their own agents in their own way, and the team loses the
plot. The work happens, but nobody can see across it.

AIOS isn't a replacement for any of that. It's the layer on top that helps a
team manage rapidly evolving context, harnessing, and tooling. You keep your
runtime, your editor, your stack. Claude, Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, whatever
you reach for. AIOS gives that work a shape the team can share.

## How it works

Each person works in a numbered folder spine: context, inbox, work, log,
shared, personal. The same six folders every time, so an agent always knows
where it is and where things go.

Everything you create carries an access tier, and the tier decides what is
private, what reaches the team, and what goes to clients or the wider company.
Nothing leaves your machine without a push.

The shared tier is where the team brain comes in. Pushed work becomes shared
memory: tasks, decisions, deliverables, all queryable in plain English across
the whole team. One place to ask what's happening, instead of ten.

## What we're shipping

A few concrete things are live today:

- **The individual workspace.** Scaffold a workspace in a few commands, with
  the folder spine, governance conventions, validators, and multi-agent
  harnesses built in.
- **The team brain.** A shared hub that receives tier-tagged pushes from every
  workspace and surfaces tasks, decisions, and deliverables, with a
  natural-language query across shared memory.
- **A sync contract between them.** The CLI and the brain build against one
  versioned API, so the two halves never quietly drift apart.

It pulls into the tools you already use: Slack, GitHub, Linear, Notion,
Granola, and more.

## Where this is going
